Hello, and thanks for visiting my webpage. I am an experienced risk analytics professional who is passionate about data science. On this page you'll find an overview of my experience and projects.

Experience

I am an experienced risk analytics professional who has spent 10 years using quantitative methods to solve business problems in finance and risk. I've been fortunate to have worked at leading global firms. I am passionate about using data to gain insight into complex situations. Nothing drives me more than seeing my team or organization benefit from the use of modern technology to gain efficiency.

I've always liked math. Mathematics and statistical models have a powerful way of explaining the world around us and they often cut through the ambiguity of business problems.

Data science excites me because it is math on hyperdrive. For 10 years I've seen how better data and even a little code can greatly enhance a business process. In the next 10 years, I'll be using the powerful data science mix of big data, coding, machine learning, and AI to tackle challenging problems at the frontier of innovation.

I work best in a culture that is collaborative and provides continuous learning opportunities.

JP Morgan

I worked in valuation control, focusing on the pricing and calculation of fair value adjustments. This included developing new pricing methodology based on regression, automating key validation processes and collaborating with many teams including trading, operations and finance. During my time at JP Morgan I developed an anomaly detection system for prices.

Total

As part of the market risk team at Total, I helped develop models for valuation of options. In addition, I analyzed and reported mark-to-market valuations, risk positions, basis changes, and profit-and-loss results. A key part of my role was to monitor trading activities and risk position of the natural gas trading group.

Citi

At Citi I was focused on applying data science to valuation in the commodities trading group. I applied clustering techniques using scikit-learn to verify and improve consensus pricing and proxy. In addition, I leveraged neural networks to create a anomaly detection system for pricing and applied regression analysis to calculate reserves and pricing for complex structured deals.

Data Science Projects

Below are some of the projects that I've worked on during my masters degree in data science at the University of California Berkeley. They cover a variety of subjects and tools. Links to the github repo with the jupyter notebook and the original data sources are provided.

Bike share SF

In this project I used Google cloud platform to determine the usage of Ford Go Bikes in SF. The main focus of the project was the usage of bikes and the level of usage during commuter hours. Visualizations were done in matplotlib.

House price prediction

In this project I used gradient boosting machines to predict house prices on a Kaggle dataset. I explored three techniques: linear regression (as a baseline), random forests and gradient boosting machines. The notebook with the analysis and results is available on the github.

Tax in the US

Using publicly available tax revenue data at the zip code level, we explored the distribution of income tax. The analysis explores which zip codes pay the highest and lowest income, which states generate the most tax revenue and contribute the highest level of per capita tax revenue.
